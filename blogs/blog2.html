First Book I Read!

Rich Dad Poor Dad is a book on finance. A great book if you are new to the world of finance. In the book Robert Kiyosaki(author of the book) share his experience of his two dads. His poor dad who worked for money, was educated but struggled with money. On the other hand his friend dad who he called his Rich dad whose money worked for him, wasn’t educated but still rich.

Here are my top 3 learning from the book.

1. Don’t work for money, make money work for you. Robert talks about how his poor dad worked for money where as his rich dad made money work for him. He always believe in paying himself first i.e putting his money to work where it will grow. On the other hand his poor dad spend most of his salary repaying his debt and other bills.

2. Understanding Asset and Liabilities On the theoretical level understanding asset and liabilities is very different from knowing how to actually use the concept. In the book, author explained Asset is anything that put money into your pocket (like stocks, bonds, real estates) and liabilities as anything that take money out from your pocket (like debt/loans) which is a great and simple way for layman to understand such financial concepts. To get rich one should try to increase the asset and decrease the liabilities from his/her life.

3. Most people act based upon emotion mainly fear or greed. People are emotional creatures. They work based on emotions. When it comes to the equity market, people operated on two emotions i.e fear and greed . When the market in the boom phase, people get greedy and put everything in the stock market and it the exact moment where smart investor take out their money and make all the profit. On the other hand at the time of bust, people the fearful. Helping smart investor to buy at discounted prices.
